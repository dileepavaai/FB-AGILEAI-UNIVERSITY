Framework Governance Charter

This charter defines the governance principles under which institutional capability frameworks are defined, maintained, versioned, and interpreted by Agile AI University.

1. Institutional Authority

Capability frameworks published by Agile AI University are issued as institutional reference models.

Framework definitions are established under academic and governance review and are not subject to ad hoc modification.

Interpretations, assessments, or derivative instruments must remain aligned to the officially defined framework version.

2. Version Integrity

Each framework maintains explicit version notation (e.g., AAOC v1.0).

Version updates are introduced deliberately and documented clearly. Changes are not made reactively in response to trends, marketing incentives, or short-term positioning.

Prior versions remain historically valid institutional references.

3. Structural Stability

Core dimensional architecture, classification thresholds, and definitional boundaries are preserved across minor revisions.

Structural changes require formal review and may result in a major version increment.

4. Interpretive Separation

Structural classification models and interpretive instruments are maintained as distinct layers.

  • The Classification Layer defines capability domains, indicators, and rule-based threshold mapping.
  • The Interpretive Layer provides contextual analysis, executive implications, or governance interpretation.

Interpretive outputs do not modify or override foundational scoring logic.

5. Boundary Preservation

To maintain institutional neutrality and long-term integrity, Agile AI University frameworks:

  • do not prescribe vendors, platforms, or implementation tools
  • do not function as certification marketing instruments
  • are not optimized for commercial positioning
  • are not modified for promotional leverage

Frameworks serve as structured academic reference systems.

6. Transparency Principle

Framework methodology, dimensional definitions, and classification logic are described publicly to ensure clarity of institutional intent.

Transparency of structure strengthens legitimacy and reduces perception risk.

7. Stewardship

Stewardship responsibility for institutional frameworks resides with the Academic and Governance authority of Agile AI University.

Stewardship includes:

  • periodic review for conceptual coherence
  • ethical boundary oversight
  • version documentation
  • interpretive consistency
