# Capability Progression Framework

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** GOVERNANCE LOCKED

**Date:** June 2026

---

# Executive Summary

The Capability Progression Framework defines how academic capabilities evolve within the Agile AI University ecosystem.

It establishes governance for academic progression while preserving the independence of university programs, credentials and learning pathways.

Capability progression shall never assume a single sequential academic pathway.

Instead, progression is determined through university-governed academic relationships.

---

# Purpose

The Capability Progression Framework exists to answer:

> What academic opportunities should a learner pursue after completing one or more university programs?

The framework provides academic governance for:

- Capability progression
- Bridge programs
- Continuing education
- Executive learning
- Professional development
- Future academic pathways

---

# Academic Principles

## Principle 1

Programs are Independent Academic Offerings.

Examples include

- Agile Outcome Practitioner (AOP)
- Artificial Intelligence Professional Agilist (AIPA)
- Agentic AI Agilist (AAIA)
- Artificial Intelligence Delegator & Enabler (AIDE)

Programs are never versions of one another.

---

## Principle 2

Capabilities represent professional competence.

Capabilities may be achieved through multiple academic pathways.

Example

A learner may obtain AIPA through

• Direct Program

or

• AOP + Bridge Program

Both pathways produce equivalent university capability.

---

## Principle 3

Credentials remain permanent academic achievements.

Capability progression never invalidates previous credentials.

Example

A learner completing

AOP

↓

Bridge Program

↓

AIPA

retains

✓ AOP Credential

✓ AIPA Credential

Both remain part of the permanent academic portfolio.

---

## Principle 4

Capability progression is graph-based.

The university does not define mandatory academic levels.

Programs may relate through

- Bridge relationships
- Recommended relationships
- Parallel pathways
- Executive pathways
- Continuing education pathways
- Future academic relationships

---

# Academic Relationship Types

The university recognizes the following academic relationships.

## Bridge

Provides a transition from one capability to another.

Example

AOP

↓

Bridge

↓

AIPA

---

## Recommended

Represents the university's recommended next learning opportunity.

Example

AIPA

↓

AAIA

---

## Parallel

Represents complementary capabilities.

Completion of one program does not require the other.

---

## Executive

Represents progression into executive education.

---

## Continuing Education

Represents professional development beyond certification.

---

## Equivalent

Represents academically equivalent university recognition.

Equivalent relationships require Academic Governance approval.

# Capability Progression Rules

Capability progression shall

✓ Preserve credential integrity

✓ Preserve academic history

✓ Support multiple learning pathways

✓ Support future programs

Capability progression shall not

✗ Replace credentials

✗ Modify credential history

✗ Assume mandatory progression

✗ Assume linear academic levels

---

# Bridge Program Governance

Bridge Programs provide academic transition opportunities.

Bridge Programs

- extend capability

- preserve historical credentials

- produce new academic recognition

Bridge Programs never replace previous university achievements.

---

# Learning Path Governance

Academic progression is represented as a graph.

Example

AOP

↓

Bridge

↓

AIPA

↓

Recommended

↓

AAIA

↓

Executive AI

The graph may branch, merge or terminate.

The framework imposes no structural limitation.

---

# Learning Opportunity Governance

Every authenticated learner may receive personalized learning opportunities.

Learning opportunities shall consider

- earned credentials

- completed programs

- university relationships

- bridge eligibility

- executive eligibility

Recommendations shall never be hardcoded inside UI components.

---

# Credential Governance

The Credential Registry remains the authoritative academic record.

Capability Progression never modifies credential records.

Capability Progression consumes credential information.

---

# Program Governance

Academic relationships belong to Program Governance.

Program metadata defines

- bridge opportunities

- recommended programs

- parallel opportunities

- executive pathways

The Learning Journey Service consumes these relationships.

---

# Portal Governance

The Student & Executive Portal consumes resolved capability progression.

The Portal never computes progression.

The Portal never determines eligibility.

The Portal renders resolver output only.

---

# Resolver Governance

The resolver owns

- capability resolution

- learning opportunities

- bridge opportunities

- recommendation generation

The resolver never renders UI.

---

# Overall Position

The Capability Progression Framework establishes a governance model that separates academic progression from application implementation.

Programs remain independent.

Credentials remain immutable.

Capability progression is graph-based.

Learning opportunities are personalized.

The framework provides a scalable academic foundation for future bridge programs, executive education, AI-driven recommendations and lifelong professional learning across the Agile AI University ecosystem.
