**Version:** 1.0

# Executive Summary

The Bridge Program Governance defines the academic, operational and platform governance for all Bridge Programs offered by Agile AI University.

Bridge Programs enable learners to transition from an existing university capability to a newer or broader capability without invalidating previous academic achievements.

Bridge Programs preserve academic integrity while supporting lifelong professional development.

This governance applies across the Student Portal, Executive Portal, Credential Operations, Learning Journey Framework and future AI-enabled learning services.

---

# Vision

Provide a structured, transparent and academically governed pathway that enables university members to continuously upgrade their professional capabilities while preserving the value of previously earned credentials.

Bridge Programs shall become the university's primary mechanism for capability evolution.

---

# Purpose

Bridge Programs exist to

- Upgrade professional capability
- Preserve previous academic achievements
- Reduce duplicate learning
- Recognize prior learning
- Support continuous professional development
- Encourage lifelong engagement with the university

Bridge Programs are capability extensions rather than replacement programs.

# Academic Principles

## Principle 1

Bridge Programs preserve academic history.

Completion of a Bridge Program shall never invalidate previously earned credentials.

Historical achievements remain permanent.

---

## Principle 2

Bridge Programs produce new academic recognition.

Successful completion results in a new university credential.

Previous credentials remain visible within the learner's portfolio.

---

## Principle 3

Bridge Programs recognize prior learning.

Bridge Programs assume learners already possess prerequisite knowledge acquired through previous university programs or academically approved equivalent pathways.

---

## Principle 4

Bridge Programs extend capability.

They do not replace programs.

They do not modify historical academic records.

---

# Bridge Program Definition

A Bridge Program is a university-approved academic pathway that enables a learner to transition from one capability to another through focused learning rather than repeating an entire program.

Example

Agile Outcome Practitioner (AOP)

↓

AOP → AIPA Bridge Program

↓

Artificial Intelligence Professional Agilist (AIPA)

---

# Eligibility Governance

Eligibility shall be determined by university governance.

Eligibility may consider

- Earned credentials
- Completed programs
- Academic relationships
- University-approved equivalencies
- Professional experience (where approved)

Eligibility shall never be determined by the user interface.

---

# Learning Path Governance

Bridge Programs are represented as academic relationships within the Learning Journey Framework.

Example

AOP

↓

Bridge

↓

AIPA

Bridge relationships are directional.

Multiple bridge opportunities may exist from the same program.

---

# Credential Governance

Bridge Programs never modify existing credentials.

Example

Before Bridge

✓ Agile Outcome Practitioner

After Bridge

✓ Agile Outcome Practitioner

✓ Artificial Intelligence Professional Agilist

Both credentials remain permanently available.

---

# Recognition Governance

Bridge completion generates

- University Credential
- University Certificate
- Digital Badge
- Recognition Assets

Recognition assets remain linked to the newly awarded credential.

---

# Capability Governance

Capability progression is determined through earned credentials.

Capability shall never be inferred from Bridge Program enrollment alone.

Enrollment does not equal completion.

Completion results in academic recognition.

---

# Learning Journey Integration

The Learning Journey Service shall identify Bridge opportunities.

The service shall determine

- Eligibility
- Available Bridge Programs
- Completed Bridges
- Future opportunities

The Student Portal consumes this information.

The Student Portal never computes eligibility.

---

Eligible learners may see

Current Achievement

↓

Bridge Opportunity

↓

Expected Capability

Bridge Benefits

↓

Registration

Bridge opportunities shall be contextual.

The portal shall never present generic promotional messages.

# Executive Portal Experience

- Executive Bridge Programs

---

# Registration Governance

Bridge registration shall remain independent from capability resolution.

Registration systems may integrate with

- CRM
- Student Information Systems
- Payment Platforms
- Enrollment Services

Registration completion shall not modify academic records.

---

# Completion Governance

Successful Bridge completion results in

- New credential issuance
- Updated learner portfolio
- Learning Journey recalculation
- Recognition asset generation

Bridge completion shall trigger standard university credential workflows.

---

# Program Governance

Bridge relationships belong to Program Governance.

Program metadata defines

- Bridge availability
- Target program
- Academic relationship
- Active status

Business logic shall not be hardcoded into portal applications.

---

# Component Governance

Future reusable components include

- Bridge Opportunity Card
- Bridge Benefits Card
- Capability Upgrade Card
- Learning Journey Card
- Registration CTA Card

Components remain independent and reusable.

---

# JavaScript Governance

JavaScript owns

- Rendering
- State management
- User interactions

---

# CSS Governance

Bridge experiences follow the Portal Design System.

All styling shall use

- Design Tokens

- Responsive Layouts

No Bridge-specific styling shall duplicate component behaviour.

---

# AI Governance

Artificial Intelligence may

- Explain Bridge Programs
- Recommend appropriate Bridge opportunities
- Answer learner questions
- Personalize learning guidance

AI shall never determine academic eligibility or award academic recognition.

Academic governance remains authoritative.

# Overall Position

The Bridge Program Governance establishes the university-wide academic framework for capability evolution across the Agile AI University ecosystem.

Bridge Programs preserve historical achievements, recognize prior learning and provide structured pathways to new capabilities without compromising academic integrity.

The Learning Journey Service, Capability Progression Framework and Student & Executive Portal collectively consume this governance to deliver personalized, graph-based learning experiences that support lifelong professional development while maintaining the university's resolver-first architecture and credential integrity.
