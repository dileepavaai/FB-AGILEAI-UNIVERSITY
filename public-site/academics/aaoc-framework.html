Institutional Framework Definition

Framework: Applied Agile AI Orchestration Capability (AAOC)
Version: v1.0
Status: Active Institutional Reference Model

1. Framework Overview

The Applied Agile AI Orchestration Capability (AAOC) Framework is a structured academic capability classification model defined and maintained by Agile AI University.

The framework evaluates how Agile execution discipline, Artificial Intelligence fluency, systemic coherence, ethical governance, and outcome-based orchestration integrate within professional and organizational environments.

AAOC is designed as a rule-based developmental classification model. It does not function as a psychometric instrument, predictive assessment, hiring filter, or behavioral scoring engine.

2. Dimensional Architecture (5 × 5 Model)

The AAOC framework is structured as a 5 × 5 capability matrix, evaluating five defined domains across five structured indicators within each domain (25 total indicators).

  • Agile Execution Discipline
    Alignment of adaptability, outcome clarity, and learning integration within execution systems.
  • AI Conceptual Fluency
    Structured understanding of AI capabilities, limitations, and responsible decision integration.
  • Systems Coherence
    Recognition of interdependencies, feedback dynamics, and long-term systemic effects.
  • Ethical Governance & Trust
    Explicit consideration of responsibility, transparency, accountability, and human impact.
  • Outcome-Based System Prompting
    Structured articulation of intent, measurable outputs, and disciplined orchestration of AI-enabled workflows.

3. Classification Methodology

Responses within AAOC-aligned instruments are interpreted using standardized threshold evaluation. Domain-level averages are mapped to structured maturity classifications:

  • Foundational – Capability characteristics are emerging, reactive, or inconsistent.
  • Emerging – Capability characteristics are visible and developing with partial integration.
  • Integrated – Capability characteristics operate coherently across execution, governance, and decision systems.

The classification reflects developmental posture rather than performance scoring.

4. Interpretive Layers

The AAOC framework operates across two distinct interpretive layers:

  • Insight Classification Layer – Structured capability description based on rule-based threshold evaluation.
  • Executive Interpretation Layer – Contextual analysis relating to governance exposure, leadership accountability, and AI-enabled decision authority.

Executive interpretation extends beyond structural classification and is maintained separately from foundational scoring logic.

5. Institutional Positioning

Agile AI University defines structured academic frameworks, capability standards, and professional recognition models within the Agile AI domain.

The AAOC framework is maintained as an institutional reference model and may evolve through formal version updates governed under established institutional review principles.

Governance principles for framework maintenance are defined in the Framework Governance Charter .
