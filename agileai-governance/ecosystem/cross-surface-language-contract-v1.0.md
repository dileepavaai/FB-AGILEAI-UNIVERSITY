# Cross-Surface Language Contract  
## Agile AI Ecosystem — Foundation · University · Academy  

Date Locked: 29 March 2026  
Status: FINAL · GOVERNANCE LOCK · NO DRIFT  
Version: v1.0  

---

# 1. Purpose

This contract ensures consistent institutional language, positioning, and semantic integrity across all ecosystem surfaces.

It prevents:

- role confusion  
- positioning drift  
- unintended commercialization  
- dilution of institutional credibility  

---

# 2. Core Language Principle

Each entity must operate within its defined semantic boundary.

Rule:

If a statement applies to multiple entities, it must be rewritten.

---

# 3. Canonical Role Definitions

## 3.1 Foundation

- Defines the domain  
- Publishes canonical frameworks  
- Establishes governance principles  

Allowed language:

- defines  
- establishes  
- formalizes  
- publishes  
- articulates  

Prohibited:

- learn  
- training  
- course  
- certification  
- enroll  

---

## 3.2 University

- Operationalizes standards  
- Assesses capability  
- Issues recognition  

Allowed:

- assesses  
- evaluates  
- analyzes  
- interprets  
- recognizes  

Prohibited:

- training  
- course delivery  
- placement  
- job guarantee  

---

## 3.3 Academy

- Enables learning  
- Builds capability  
- Supports execution  

Allowed:

- learn  
- apply  
- practice  
- develop capability  
- scenario-based  

Prohibited:

- define standards  
- certify authority  
- official evaluation  
- tool-based training  

---

# 4. Boundary Rules

- Foundation must not imply learning  
- University must not imply training  
- Academy must not imply authority  

---

# 5. Directional Flow

Allowed:

Foundation → University → Academy  

Not allowed:

- Academy redefining standards  
- Reverse authority flow  

---

# 6. CTA Governance

Foundation:

- No promotional CTAs  

University:

- Assessment access allowed  
- No learning CTAs  

Academy:

- Learning CTAs allowed  

---

# 7. Terminology Lock

Always use:

Agile AI  

Not allowed:

- Agile and AI  
- AI + Agile  

---

# 8. Standard Descriptions

Foundation:

Agile AI Foundation defines canonical frameworks and operates as a neutral, non-commercial authority layer.

University:

Agile AI University defines structured academic frameworks, capability standards, and professional recognition models.

Academy:

Agile AI Academy enables structured learning and applied capability development.
