# Purpose

This document establishes the official motion and animation standards for Agile AI University.

Motion should enhance usability by providing visual feedback, reinforcing hierarchy and communicating state changes.

Motion exists to support interaction—not to entertain.

---

# Scope

This specification applies to:

* Public Website
* Student & Executive Portal
* Administration Portal
* Credential Operations Suite
* Credential Registry
* Certificate Generator
* Badge Generator
* Credential Verification
* Recognition Services
* Future Products

---

# Motion Philosophy

Motion shall be:

* purposeful
* subtle
* professional
* predictable
* accessible

Animations should improve understanding without distracting users.

---

# Motion Principles

## Purpose First

Every animation should communicate something meaningful.

Examples:

* state change
* focus
* expansion
* loading
* completion
* navigation

---

## Minimalism

Animations should remain restrained.

Large or decorative animations are discouraged.

---

## Consistency

Identical interactions should produce identical animations throughout the platform.

---

## Performance

Animations should be hardware accelerated where practical.

Avoid expensive layout recalculations.

---

# Approved Motion Types

The platform supports:

* Fade
* Slide
* Scale
* Expand
* Collapse
* Hover Lift
* Loading Spinner
* Progress Indicator

Additional motion types require governance approval.

---

# Hover Effects

Hover interactions should be subtle.

Examples include:

* slight elevation
* gentle shadow
* soft color transition

Excessive movement is prohibited.

---

# Focus Effects

Focus indicators shall remain visible.

Motion should never replace the required focus outline.

---

# Buttons

Button interactions may include:

* slight elevation
* subtle shadow
* opacity transition

Buttons should never bounce, rotate or perform decorative animations.

---

# Cards

Cards may:

* elevate slightly on hover
* increase shadow subtly
* maintain stable layout

Cards should not shift excessively.

---

# Navigation

Navigation transitions should be smooth and predictable.

Dropdowns and mobile menus may:

* fade
* slide

Navigation should never animate unnecessarily.

---

# Dialogs

Dialogs may:

* fade in
* scale slightly
* fade out

Dialog transitions should remain brief.

---

# Accordions

Accordion panels should expand and collapse smoothly.

Content should remain readable during the transition.

---

# Notifications

Notifications may:

* fade
* slide into view
* fade away

Dismissal should feel smooth without distracting movement.

---

# Loading States

Loading indicators should communicate progress.

Approved loading patterns include:

* spinner
* skeleton screen
* progress bar

Skeleton screens are preferred for page loading.

---

# Timing

Recommended durations:

| Interaction     | Duration  |
| --------------- | --------- |
| Hover           | 150–200ms |
| Focus           | 150ms     |
| Button          | 150–200ms |
| Card            | 200ms     |
| Dialog          | 200–250ms |
| Navigation      | 200–250ms |
| Page Transition | 250–350ms |

Long animations are discouraged.

---

# Easing

Preferred easing:

* ease
* ease-in-out

Complex easing curves should be avoided unless justified.

---

# Reduced Motion

The platform shall respect user preferences for reduced motion.

When reduced motion is enabled:

* minimize transitions
* remove decorative animations
* preserve usability

---

# Accessibility

Motion shall never:

* obscure content
* interfere with reading
* trigger unnecessary movement
* delay user interaction

Accessibility takes precedence over visual effects.

---

# Performance

Motion should prioritize:

* transform
* opacity

Avoid animating properties that trigger expensive layout or paint operations unless necessary.

---

# Governance Rules

1. Motion shall have a functional purpose.

2. Decorative animation is discouraged.

3. Motion shall remain subtle and professional.

4. Durations shall follow this specification.

5. Components shall behave consistently across products.

6. Reduced motion preferences shall be respected.

7. Motion shall not interfere with accessibility.

8. New animation patterns require governance approval.

9. Motion shall support—not distract from—the user experience.

10. Every product shall comply with these Motion Guidelines.
