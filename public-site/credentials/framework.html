Credential Framework

Credentials issued by Agile AI University represent formal professional recognition aligned to defined capability standards and governance frameworks.

Credentialing is governed independently and is not automatically granted through assessments, attendance, or participation.

Purpose of Credentialing

The purpose of credentialing is to:

  • formally recognize advanced professional capability
  • protect professional and institutional credibility
  • avoid dilution of professional standards
  • ensure consistency across issued recognitions

Credentials are designed to serve employers, professionals, and the broader ecosystem â€” not short-term commercial objectives.

Separation from Capability Assessment

Open capability assessments published by Agile AI University are designed as insight and reflection instruments.

They do not:

  • confer credentials
  • imply professional status
  • serve as eligibility screening
  • trigger automatic enrollment

This separation preserves academic integrity and protects professionals from misleading claims.

Credential Eligibility

Credentials may be issued only under approved professional tracks and governed programs.

Eligibility is evaluated based on:

  • demonstrated professional experience
  • capability alignment with defined standards
  • completion of approved learning or validation pathways
  • adherence to ethical and professional expectations

Eligibility criteria are applied deliberately, not automatically.

Credential Governance

All credentials are governed under formally approved academic and equivalency frameworks.

Governance provisions include:

  • defined scope and intent of each credential
  • issuance and approval controls
  • review and audit mechanisms
  • revocation and correction policies

Governance ensures that credentials retain long-term professional value.

Verification & Authenticity

Credentials issued by Agile AI University may be verified through official verification channels.

Verification confirms:

  • credential authenticity
  • issuance status
  • governing framework alignment

Open capability assessments do not require credential verification.

Professional Responsibility

Credential holders are expected to:

  • represent credentials accurately
  • avoid misrepresentation or exaggeration
  • act in alignment with ethical standards
  • respect the boundaries of credential scope

Misuse or misrepresentation may result in review or revocation.
